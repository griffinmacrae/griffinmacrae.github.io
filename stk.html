Overview

This STK analysis was used to calculate the link budget of a hypothetical data relay Satellite constellation. There were two objectives. The first was to compare two different types of photodetectors in the receivers to determine which one fits the mission objective better. Requirements and considerations

Transmitter Requirements

  • Data rate > 1 Gb/s
  • Used for space mission i.e. TRL 9
  • Range > 73400 km
  • Lifetime > 10 years
  • Data rate > 1.8 Gb/s
  • Large functional temperature range
  • Low cost
  • Low mass and size
  • Precise pointing requirements for longer range
  • High jitter control

Receiver Requirements

  • Data rate > 1 Gb/s
  • Used for space mission i.e. TRL 9
  • Low mass and size
  • Range > 73400 km
  • Lifetime > 10 years
  • Minimal power since there will be 10 per satellite
  • Large functional temperature range
  • Low cost
  • High jitter control
  • Precise pointing requirements for longer range

Objects and Assumptions

Additional Note: Receiver and transmitter specs are based off the actual receivers and transmitters. The sensor is based off the half-cone angle of an optical laser which is 1 degree. The satellite is roughly based of the TRDS-13, but the specs are so different (optical vs RF) that a custom satellite was used instead. The orbit simulation time is 24 hours.
