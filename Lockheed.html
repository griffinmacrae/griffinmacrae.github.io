Lockheed Martin

I worked on three main projects as an Aeronautical Engineering intern at Lockheed Martin. I can provide high level information about these projects and my role. However, most details are proprietary. Also, as part of this position I received DoD Secret Security Clearance.

I worked on an aerodynamic harness as part of the GNC team on a newer project. The purpose of this harness was to validate aerodynamic data coming from two different models so that 6dof simulation templates could be plug and play for future projects. I used Git to add my updates to the model and we utilized GitLab for assigning tasks and giving updates. I attended daily scrum sessions so we would achieve all our sprint goals. I made this harness in Matlab / Simulink. I also utilized Lockheed Martin Matlab add-ins to import aeroydamic data into the model.

I worked as a research engineer in a wet lab. I worked on bio-inspired materials. In this materials lab I learned how to use wet lab equipment, procedures, and used critical thinking skills to come up with novel better solutions which are cheaper. I also read literature to base these decisions off of along with writing research proposals based off my research. Some applications include corrosion resistance, and ablative materials.

I worked as a GNC engineer on a consultation project. I created high level GNC materials for our customer. This included timelines, block diagrams, and hardware/software integration. I also was tasked with liasoning between two different business areas leading to an improvement in my leadership and communication skills. I also learned more about the GNC subsystem from a high level overview. *Unfortunately I'm unable to share any pictures from my time at Lockheed Martin.
