Mechoptronics

Mechoptronics is an engineering lab I took. It's a mix of mechanical, electronic, and optics. I learned how to use lab equipment including a power supply, digital multimeter, digital/virtual oscilloscopes, and waveform generators. I also used LabView to collect data later analyzed and converted into useful results using Matlab.

One lab was done on SMA (Shape Memory Alloy) springs which I later applied to a project called STARFISH I worked on. I calculated the efficiency of these springs with different loads and with different applied voltage.

Another lab was done on airfoils. I compared a two different airfoils, one symmetric and one assymetric and analyzed how different Reynolds numbers would affect their lift and drag coefficients. The data was collected using a wind tunnel.

Another lab was done on turbines. I found the boundary layer of a jet turbine and found at what speeds and locations the flow would turn from laminar into turbulent flow and at what Reynolds numbers.

Another lab was done on the heat transfer of different thermal paints for spacecraft applications. The purpose was to determine how different thermal paints can be used on spacecraft to keep components within their thermal operating temperatures.
