Hello, my name is Griffin MacRae. I'm currently a senior/masters student at the University of Southern California studying Astronautical Engineering (B.S. and M.S.). I have a background in various engineering positions.

I've been working at USC's Space Engineering Research Center (SERC) for over two years. At my time at SERC I've worked on several projects with different roles. I worked on a soft robot (STARFISH) as the lead design engineer designing the limb this robot will use to locomote. This research has since been published. I worked on a genderless docking system called CLINGERS as a mechanical engineer. I worked as a mechanical and assembly engineer working on the safety features NASA gave us. I assembled the flight hardware in a clean room. I also created the MEL and BOM of this 1000+ part assembly. I also created a structure to hold the S-Band electronics for the ground station at USC. I worked on a bioinspired space debris robot as an electronics and controls engineer. I created a schematic for this project in KiCad and did some of the programming for the controls. I also worked on machining an adaptor for a docking system.

I interned at Lockheed Martin where I was an Aeronautical Engineering Intern. I had several responsibilities and worked in a few different fields. I worked in a materials lab looking at novel materials and writing funding proposals. I worked as a part of a GNC team developing an aerodynamic test harness in Simulink to validate aerodynamic data. I also worked as part of a conceptual design team providing high level GNC delivarables to a customer.
